Immune2V Protection

Immune2V Protection. Immune2V protects images from Image-to-Video (I2V) generation by adding imperceptible immunization noise to produce an immunized image. When processed by an I2V generator, a clean image yields realistic motion (e.g., the human face articulates naturally and the train moves circularly), whereas the immunized image disrupts the generation dynamics, producing highly unrealistic videos (e.g., the human subject is severely distorted and the train structurally collapses).

Immunization on Dual-Stream I2V Architecture

Immunization on Dual-Stream I2V Architecture. A clean input image guides the generation process through (i) a spatial-temporal stream, where the image is processed through a video encoder to initialize the structural latent space, and (ii) a semantic stream, where an image encoder extracts high-level embeddings for continuous semantic guidance. While these streams normally generate a realistic video, injecting an adversarial perturbation yields an immunized image that simultaneously disrupts both conditioning streams, forcing the generation process into structural collapse.

Semantic Conditioning Override

Semantic Conditioning Override. We analyze generative trajectories in the noise latent space by measuring semantic distance to a clean baseline at each timestep. (a) A mismatched prompt (Bad Prompt) naturally diverges from the baseline. (b) An encoder-only spatial attack is overridden by the continuous semantic guidance of a good prompt, rapidly pulling the trajectory back toward the clean state. (c) Our joint approach actively counteracts the semantic guidance, maintaining a flatter slope and sustaining the disruption across the generative process.

Qualitative Comparisons

Qualitative Comparisons. A qualitative comparison across baselines, showing the input frame and subsequent video frames. Clean inputs and baselines yield coherent videos with plausible motion, while our approach induces progressive structural collapse (e.g., the BMX rider's geometry distorts mid-sequence, and the horse rider loses anatomical coherence) that persists across frames. Notably, PhotoGuard-E leaves visible perturbation traces on the input image due to its fast-converging encoder-only loss, yet this corruption fails to transfer to the generated video.
